Introduction

Traditional Oil sticks have the benefit of being permanent once its dried. Soft pastels are better for blending, and you can use a soft haired brush to do gentler blending techniques. This recipe allows you to create something like Oil Sticks, but with blending aspects closer to soft pastels. But with the drying characteristics of linseed oil.

Recipe

2/3 Cup Of Chalk
1 Cup Of Charcoal or Graphite
Pigment of your choosing.
2/3 Cup Emulsion of Isopropyl, Rice Vinegar, and Linseed Oil
1 tablespoon Gum Tragacanth

Dissolve pigment in rubbing alcohol in a bowl, athen add an emulsion of rubbing alcohol, rice vinegar, and linseed oil. Mix with chalk or activated charcoal enough to form a past like consistency, and thicker for more compact pastels.

Blend paste with 1 tablespoon of Gum tragacanth. Carefully mix this with a hand spatula until there is is minimal air bubbles, sort of like clay. And thick enough to mold into bars.

Now mold these into relatively thick bars. And allow to rest for 3-4 days.

Intended Application

This is intended for create a look more like Sumi Ink rather than traditional western painting. With the advantage that you can take it on the go. Note that you can also use Rabbit Skin glue if you want your soft sturdier, as its not as stingy about pigment as gum tragacanth.
